1. ## Introduction	 {#introduction}

As a Student Digital Ambassador at VIT Bhopal, I have developed this portfolio to demonstrate my proficiency in navigating the modern digital landscape. Digital literacy is a core competency for any Engineer, particularly in the field of Cloud Computing and Automation. This project cov- ers five essential modules: creating visual educational resources, building a professional online identity, mastering collaboration tools, practicing professional communication, and analyzing cyber threats. Each task in this report reflects a blend of technical skill and responsible digital citizenship.

2. ## Task 1: Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic	 {#task-1:-digital-literacy-awareness-infographic}

   1. Overview

For this task, I designed a one-page visual resource using Canva to educate my peers on essen- tial digital skills. The infographic focuses on three core pillars: Online Safety, Essential Digital Tools, and Email Etiquette.

2. Reflection

For the first task of my Digital Literacy portfolio, I designed a one-page infographic aimed at educating my peers on the essential components of navigating the modern digital landscape. I utilized Canva, a browser-based graphic design tool, to create a visually engaging and easy- to-read resource. My design specifically focuses on three core pillars of digital literacy: Safe Internet Practices, Useful Digital Tools for Students, and Professional Email Etiquette.

One thing I found particularly interesting while making this infographic was the challenge of ”visual hierarchy”—learning how to arrange information so that the most critical safety tips catch the reader’s eye first. Balancing aesthetic appeal with concise, actionable information was a valuable exercise in digital communication.

   1. Overview

This module focused on professional communication protocols, including drafting formal aca- demic and professional emails and defining social media boundaries.

2. Reflection

I learned that digital communication is a critical skill for a Student Digital Ambassador. I drafted two professional emails, focusing on maintaining a respectful tone and clear structure, which are essential for academic and career success. I also created a social media checklist to promote responsible online behavior. One situation where poor digital communication caused a real problem was a hypothetical scenario where a student used an unprofessional email address and informal language to contact a recruiter. 6. ## Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness & Prevention	 {#task-5:-cybercrime-awareness-&-prevention}

   1. Overview

I conducted a case study on UPI/Online Payment fraud, a prevalent threat in the modern Indian financial ecosystem, and compiled a list of preventative measures.

2. Reflection

Researching UPI fraud for Task 5 was eye-opening. What surprised me most was how fraud- sters exploit a simple misunderstanding of technology—specifically the fact that a PIN is never needed to receive money. This psychological manipulation is often more effective than techni- cal hacking. As a result of this research, I will personally change my habit of scanning QR codes sent by strangers and will now double-check the ’Request’ vs. ’Pay’ labels on my UPI app every time. This task reinforced my role as a Digital Ambassador, as sharing these simple tips can prevent significant financial loss for my peers.
